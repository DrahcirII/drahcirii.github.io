The Requiem Project

Requiem is a concept concieved as a future group to promote creativity and free will to the public. The idea is to add change to a society of the few by not only augmenting the thinking of the individual, but by encouraging the spread of ideas. The mission as a group of free thinkers that sail the international waters of the net, is finding the true meaning of freedom through design and self-expression. We liberate thus burying old traditions and norms to usher forth a new generation.

Solace among the darkness, freedom above conformity!

Creator/Creation Dilemma

First off, this is my opinion and THIS IS NOT IN ANY WAY RELIGIOUS. Got a comment or remark about the religious aspects of this post? Take that crap to youtube.I can't really put my finger on a proper introduction for such a topic, my plan was to start off light and then jump into the heart of the matter, but I guess I'll just start.

Many people are familiar with ideas and how the laws of ownership apply to them. I came up with something, so it's mine. You are the creator and the idea is the creation. It's nice to own things, but does the same concept that applies to ideas or other forms of creations hold true when dealing with people or other intelligent beings? Let's add some color to that, does something that had no say in its arrival or existence bound to the will of their creator?

I personally say, "to hell with the creator!" for if I was the creation, I certainly didn't get a say in my being in existence. I think that as compensation for being clearly robbed of free will from the moment of conception one should have free reign over what they do with their existence. Plainly put, creators with expectations is a common and simple thought, but they should throw their expectations out of the window and screw themselves...quite thoroughly. Many people would say that that is a classic case of inconsideration and is a childish view of the subject - defiance even! Seriously though, if the creator brought the idea into being with the mindset of of expecting something in return, then they are asking to be flung off of a balcony amidst an exploding Death Star!

Creating a someone (even if it is a robot with emotion) is different than creating a something. I think that a creator should approach concepting a creation like rolling a dice rather than ordering a pizza. With dice, you'll come to accept the outcome and not demand explanations or even allegiance. I guarantee you that treating your creations like pizza and expecting all the toppings you asked for is just asking for them to get your order wrong. Well, this is the vampire's first installment of the "Rant Panic" series! I didn't exactly convey the anger I've racked up from my own experiences with the dilemma, but trust me THIS IS MOST DEFINITELY A RANT. See you next time in the wonderland dear readers and remember today's lesson - some effing perspective

Wait...I actually started off quite well! Ok seriously, see ya you stardust being!
